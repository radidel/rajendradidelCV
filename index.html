A Kubernetes Story: Benny Goes to the Zoo

Once upon a time in the world of technology, there lived a curious little creature named Benny. Benny wasn't like the other animals in the forest; Benny was a container. Benny lived in a tiny container village nestled between the towering trees of the Cloud Forest.

One sunny morning, Benny woke up with a sense of adventure.

“I’m bored Aunt Benny,” Zara further down on the couch.

“What are we going to do today?”

“Why not go see the animals?” said Benny with a smile. “We’ll go to the zoo!”

“Yeah!” Zara let out a whoop and ran to find some shoes.

It had heard tales of a magical place called the Kubernetes Zoo. The Kubernetes Zoo was known far and wide as the most amazing place for containers to live and thrive. Benny decided it was time to pay the zoo a visit.

The first animals they came upon were the size of squirrels. Furry and blue, each little animal carried a tiny box as they unceasingly zipped back and forth.

“Those,” said Benny, “are Pods. All day and all night, they run back and forth carrying their little containers.”

“Is that all they do, Aunt Benny?”

“Yup, Zara. For their entire lives, that’s all the Pods do. They run.”

In Kubernetes, Pods are responsible for running your containers. Every Pod holds at least one container, and controls the execution of that container. When the containers exit, the Pod dies too.

Benny and Zara found a bunch of meerkats behind glass. These meerkats were like "ReplicaSets" in Kubernetes.

One meerkat on the right jumped off, and the others quickly moved to fill the gap. Then, a new meerkat appeared on the left. This happened every time one meerkat left.

Benny explained, "Just like these meerkats, ReplicaSets in Kubernetes always make sure there are a certain number of identical pods running. If one pod stops working, Kubernetes replaces it with a new one. It's all about keeping things running smoothly."

Zara got it. "So, when one meerkat goes, another comes in, like a team. Cool!" Benny and Zara walked away, understanding how Kubernetes ensures things stay reliable, just like those clever meerkats.

  • A Deployment is a higher-order abstraction that controls deploying and maintaining a set of Pods. Behind the scenes, it uses a ReplicaSet to keep the Pods running, but it offers sophisticated logic for deploying, updating, and scaling a set of Pods within a cluster.

    • Benny and Zara came across a grassy knoll with several tall stone pillars, each hosting a vulture on its top. As they observed, one vulture spread its wings and flew away into the distance. Surprisingly, as soon as one vulture left, another one quickly landed to take its place.

    Curious, Zara asked, "What are they doing?"

    Benny explained, "Those are like DaemonSets in Kubernetes. They make sure to always have a vulture on every pillar, no matter what the weather or time of day is."

    Benny grinned and added, "I'm willing to bet that if we added a new pillar, a new vulture would land on it faster than you could say 'cube cuddle.'"

    Just like those vultures reliably occupy every pillar, DaemonSets in Kubernetes ensure that a specific kind of pod is running on every node in a cluster, regardless of any changes or disruptions.

  • DaemonSets provide a way to ensure that a copy of a Pod is running on every node in the cluster. As a cluster grows and shrinks, the DaemonSet spreads these specially labeled Pods across all of the nodes.
